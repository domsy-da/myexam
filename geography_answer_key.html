Geography Examination - Answer Key

I. Enumeration (2 points each item)

  1. 1. What are the five themes of geography? Location, Place, Region, Human-environment interactions, Movement
  2. 2. What are the two main branches of geography? Physical Geography, Human Geography
  3. 3. Name the three main layers of the Earth's structure. Crust, Mantle, Core
  4. 4. List four of the Earth's oceans. Pacific Ocean, Atlantic Ocean, Indian Ocean, Southern/Antarctic Ocean, Arctic Ocean (any 4)
  5. 5. Name the seven continents of the world. Asia, Africa, North America, South America, Antarctica, Europe, Australia
  6. 6. List three unique features of the Earth. Supports a breathable atmosphere, Contains liquid water, Has a magnetic field
  7. 7. What are the four most spoken languages listed? Mandarin, English, Spanish, Hindu
  8. 8. What are the four major religions listed? Christianity, Islam, Hinduism, Buddhism
  9. 9. Name four significant lines of latitude. Tropic of Cancer, Tropic of Capricorn, Arctic Circle, Antarctic Circle
  10. 10. What are the four inner planets? Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars

II. Identification (2 points each)

  1. 1. A person who specializes in geography. Geographer
  2. 2. The Greek word meaning "description of the earth." geographia
  3. 3. The thinnest and outermost layer of the Earth. Crust
  4. 4. The world's highest mountain, located in China and Nepal. Mount Everest
  5. 5. The world's longest river, located in Africa. Nile
  6. 6. The 0-degree latitude line that divides the Earth into the Northern and Southern Hemispheres. Equator
  7. 7. The 0-degree longitude line that divides the Earth into the Eastern and Western Hemispheres. Prime Meridian
  8. 8. The world's largest lake, found in Asia-Europe. Caspian Sea
  9. 9. The world's longest mountain range, located in South America. Andes
  10. 10. The first person in history to use a telescope for astronomical observation. Galileo Galilei
  11. 11. The natural satellite of Earth. The Moon
  12. 12. The term for large landmasses on the surface of the Earth. Continents

III. Multiple Choice (2 points each)

  1. 1. Which discipline is affected by geographical conditions determining how historical events happened? c) History
  2. 2. What is the approximate distance of Earth from the sun? b) 149.6 million km
  3. 3. What is the shape of the Earth? c) Oblate Spheroid
  4. 4. How much of the Earth's surface is covered by water? c) 71%
  5. 5. What is the diameter of the Earth? a) 12,742 km
  6. 6. How many languages are spoken today, according to the document? c) 6,800
  7. 7. What is the period of Earth's rotation? c) 23 hours, 56 minutes and 4 seconds
  8. 8. What is the definition of the global economy according to the document? b) Based on free market capitalism
  9. 9. What are horizontal lines that run east to west around the earth called? b) Parallels
  10. 10. The Prime Meridian passes through which location? c) Greenwich, a borough of London
  11. 11. What is the densest and hottest layer of semi-solid rock below the crust? c) Mantle
  12. 12. The study of the different physical features and processes of the earth is known as what? c) Physical Geography
  13. 13. How many countries were there after the Second World War, according to the document? b) 80
  14. 14. The magnetic field of the Earth protects it from what? b) Solar radiation
  15. 15. What does the term "geographia" mean? c) "Description of the earth"
  16. 16. Which of the following is the world's widest and driest desert? b) Sahara
  17. 17. The belief that the Earth was the center of the universe with other celestial bodies orbiting it was held for most of human history. Due to this belief, what was the Earth not originally considered to be? a) A planet
